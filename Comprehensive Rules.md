## Mao

1. Golden Rules: <br>
	*"Mao has many rules. The only rule that may be revealed is this one."*

	[1.1] Every action is forbidden except those that are explicitly permitted.

	[1.2] If a forbidden action is taken, any cards involved in the action are moved to the offender's hand. <br>
		  The offending player must also draw a card.

	[1.3] The State may do what it pleases.

2. Cards: <br>
	*Cards are the primary game object of Mao.*

	[2.1] A card is any object which has all of the following:
			a [Name] (it need not be unique)
   			a "front" and "back" face (these are designated when added to the game)

   	[2.2] A card may also have any amount of the following:
   			Rules Text
   			Tags

3. Basic Operations: <br>
   	*Listed here are standard actions that will be used commonly in Rules Text.*

	Draw: To draw, take the top card of the Draw Pile and add it to your Hand.
	Play: To play, take a card and put it on the top of the Play Pile.
	Pass: To pass, indicate in some way that you are passing. It stops being your turn and the next player begins their turn.
	Tuck: To tuck, take a card and put it on the bottom of the Draw Pile.
	Return: To return, take a card and put it on the top of the Draw Pile.
	Reveal: To reveal, choose a card and make it public to all players.

4. Rules Text: <br>
	*Mao has specific ways in which it words its rules.*

	[4.1] Any Rules Text in The State's [Rules Zone] applies to all players at all times.
	[4.2] Most Rules Text is to be written in the second person. "You" or "Your" are used in the singular, unless otherwise stated. 

5. Basic Rules of Play: <br>
	*These rules are by default active at the start of the game.*

	[5.1] If it is your turn and you have not played a card, you may play a card from your hand.
	[5.2] If it is your turn and you have not drawn a card, you may draw a card.
	[5.3] If it is your turn and you have drawn or played a card, you may pass.

	[5.4] You may play cards from your hand with the same name as the card on the top of the Play Pile.

	[5.5] If you pass and you have no cards in your hand:
   		Add 1 to your Score.
   		You may either:
   			Create a new Rule and add it to either the Rules Zone or your Hand.
			Or add a card from the Rules Zone to your Hand.
		Draw X cards, where X is 3 + your Score.

	[5.6] If you pass and you have 10 or more cards in your hand:
		Subtract 1 from your Score.
		Tuck half your hand (rounding up) at random.
